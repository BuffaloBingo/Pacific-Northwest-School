Famous nature-made sites

Thor's Well

Thor's Well, a natural wonder nestled along the dramatic coastline of Oregon, captivates visitors with its mesmerizing display of nature's power and beauty. Located within the Cape Perpetua Scenic Area, this geological marvel is a collapsed sea cave that forms a seemingly bottomless sinkhole. As waves crash against the rocky shoreline, water rushes into the gaping hole, creating a spectacular display of surging seawater that appears to drain into the depths of the earth. Especially awe-inspiring during high tide or stormy weather, the sight of water cascading into the abyss evokes a sense of wonder and reverence for the forces of nature. Surrounding Thor's Well is a rugged landscape of rocky cliffs and tidal pools, offering adventurers the chance to explore and discover the diverse marine life that thrives in this dynamic ecosystem. Visitors often gather at this natural amphitheater, cameras in hand, to witness the ebb and flow of the ocean's energy, mesmerized by the rhythmic dance of waves and the timeless beauty of Thor's Well. As a symbol of nature's raw power and eternal presence, Thor's Well invites all who behold it to contemplate the mysteries of the natural world and to marvel at the wonders that abound along Oregon's breathtaking coastline.


Crater lake

Crater Lake, nestled in the Cascade Range of Oregon, is renowned for its stunning beauty and natural grandeur. Formed over 7,700 years ago by the collapse of Mount Mazama, it's the deepest lake in the United States and boasts an otherworldly blue hue that captivates visitors. Rimmed by steep cliffs towering over 2,000 feet high, the lake's pristine waters reflect the surrounding cliffs and sky, creating a mesmerizing spectacle. With its unique geological history, crystal-clear waters, and picturesque vistas, Crater Lake National Park offers a haven for outdoor enthusiasts, inviting them to explore its hiking trails, scenic overlooks, and rich biodiversity.


Smith Rock State Park

Smith Rock State Park, situated in central Oregon's high desert, is a paradise for outdoor adventurers and nature lovers alike. Its striking landscape, characterized by towering cliffs of volcanic tuff and the winding Crooked River below, offers breathtaking vistas at every turn. Renowned as a premier destination for rock climbing, Smith Rock attracts climbers from around the globe with its challenging routes and iconic formations such as Monkey Face. Beyond climbing, the park's extensive network of hiking and mountain biking trails meanders through rugged terrain, showcasing the region's diverse flora and fauna. Whether scaling the cliffs, exploring the trails, or simply soaking in the awe-inspiring scenery, visitors to Smith Rock State Park are sure to be enchanted by its natural beauty and adventurous spirit.
