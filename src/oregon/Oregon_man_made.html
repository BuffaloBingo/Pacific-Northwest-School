Man-made sites

Yaquina Head Lighthouse

The Yaquina Head Lighthouse, a historic landmark on Oregon's central coast, commands attention with its towering presence, standing 93 feet above sea level. Its classic white cylindrical tower, adorned with a spiraling black stripe, is crowned by a distinctive black lantern room, a beacon of maritime history dating back to its construction in 1873. For over a century, this lighthouse has faithfully guided ships through the perilous waters of the Pacific Ocean, its powerful beam of light piercing through the darkness of night. Situated within the picturesque Yaquina Head Outstanding Natural Area, visitors are treated to panoramic views of the rugged coastline, where tide pools teem with marine life and cliffs are relentlessly pounded by the crashing waves. Beyond its practical function, the lighthouse symbolizes Oregon's rich maritime heritage, inviting travelers to explore its grounds, climb its tower for breathtaking vistas, and immerse themselves in its timeless charm and scenic beauty.


Portland Japanese Garden

The Portland Japanese Garden, nestled in the scenic hills of Portland, Oregon, is a serene oasis of tranquility and cultural beauty. Renowned as one of the most authentic Japanese gardens outside of Japan, it offers visitors a harmonious blend of meticulously manicured landscapes, traditional architecture, and peaceful water features. Designed to evoke a sense of serenity and contemplation, the garden features distinct areas such as the Strolling Pond Garden, Tea Garden, and Sand and Stone Garden, each with its own unique charm and symbolism. From the vibrant colors of cherry blossoms in spring to the vibrant hues of maple leaves in fall, the garden's ever-changing seasonal landscape captivates visitors year-round, inviting them to immerse themselves in the timeless beauty and peaceful ambiance of Japanese culture.


OMSI (Oregon Museum of Science and Industry)

The Oregon Museum of Science and Industry (OMSI) stands as a beacon of curiosity and exploration in Portland, Oregon, captivating visitors of all ages with its fascinating exhibits, interactive displays, and engaging educational programs. Set along the scenic Willamette River, OMSI's sprawling campus is a testament to the wonders of science, technology, and innovation. From hands-on experiments and immersive simulations to awe-inspiring planetarium shows and captivating live demonstrations, the museum offers a diverse range of experiences that ignite the imagination and spark a lifelong love of learning. Inside its modern facilities, visitors can delve into a myriad of scientific disciplines, from biology and chemistry to physics and astronomy, discovering the mysteries of the natural world and the marvels of human ingenuity. Whether exploring the inner workings of the human body in the Life Sciences Hall, embarking on a virtual journey through space and time in the Kendall Planetarium, or delving into the world of sustainable energy and environmental stewardship in the Earth Science Hall, OMSI invites guests to embark on an unforgettable voyage of discovery. Beyond its exhibits, OMSI also serves as a hub for community engagement and scientific inquiry, hosting workshops, lectures, and special events that foster creativity, critical thinking, and collaboration. With its commitment to education, innovation, and exploration, OMSI continues to inspire generations of curious minds, igniting a passion for discovery and a deeper understanding of the world we inhabit.
