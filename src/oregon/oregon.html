Population of Oregon

Oregon's population in 2024 is approximately 4,227,337 people. In terms of the ratio of females to males, 50.5% females and 49.5% males is where the population currently stands.

Oregon state animal

The Oregon state animal is the beaver (Castor canadensis). Chosen for its industrious nature and significant historical importance in the state, the beaver symbolizes Oregon's pioneering spirit and its rich natural heritage. These aquatic mammals are known for their remarkable engineering skills, constructing elaborate dams and lodges that shape their surrounding environment. Their presence in Oregon's waterways not only influences ecosystems but also reflects the interconnectedness of wildlife and landscapes in the region. As a symbol, the beaver embodies resilience, resourcefulness, and the enduring bond between Oregonians and their natural surroundings.

Man-made sites

Yaquina Head Lighthouse

Perched atop Oregon's rugged coast, Yaquina Head Lighthouse has stood since 1873, guiding ships with its classic design and striking presence. Inside, visitors step into history, climbing to the lantern room for sweeping views of the Pacific. Surrounded by wildlife and coastal beauty, it's a timeless symbol of maritime heritage.
